### Deep Dive Analysis

The Champions League knockout stage is a single-elimination tournament, where teams are matched against each other in a best-of-two format. The winner of each tie advances to the next round, while the loser is eliminated. The format is designed to test a team's resilience, adaptability, and tactical acumen.

To progress to the last 16, teams must secure a tie-winner's spot. This means that they need to accumulate more points than their opponents in the two-legged tie. The points system is as follows:

* Win: 3 points
* Draw: 1 point
* Loss: 0 points

The top two teams in each group will qualify for the last 16, while the third-placed team will enter the play-offs. The permutations for each team are as follows:

#### Liverpool

* Must win against Tottenham to secure a tie-winner's spot
* Must accumulate more points than Atletico Madrid in the process

#### Manchester City

* Must win against Borussia Dortmund to secure a tie-winner's spot
* Must accumulate more points than Sevilla in the process

#### Tottenham

* Must win against Liverpool to secure a tie-winner's spot
* Must accumulate more points than Atalanta in the process

#### Chelsea

* Must win against Lille to secure a tie-winner's spot
* Must accumulate more points than Zenit in the process

#### Newcastle

* Must win against Braga to secure a tie-winner's spot
* Must accumulate more points than Spartak Moscow in the process
